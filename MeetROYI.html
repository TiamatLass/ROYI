What is ROYI?

ROYI is the acronym for "Return on your investment". The ROYI of your meeting comes from the value your meeting provides to the meeting stakeholder(s).

There are many factors to working out the ROYI of a meeting:

  • meeting length or duration
  • the expected meeting outcome
  • the method used to teach participants
  • the type of meeting (on site, video etc)
  • the number of participants, and their expected outcomes

Why would I use ROYI?

ROYI's aim is to guide you to have less meetings that are more productive. To create time and space to enable creativity and innovation. He helps you understand:

  • the return on your investment
  • how much your cost centre can expect to be paid (or pay out) from holding the meeting

ROYI also gives you nicely formatted calendar event details to help all participants understand:

  • the expected meeting outcome
  • the expected outcomes (a.k.a potential value) for themselves
  • how to attend the meeting

Why does ROYI work in dollars?

ROYI works in "funny money" to help you:

  • see the impact of your meeting
  • think about who you REALLY want to invite, and who will actually benefit or provide value

How does ROYI get the meeting cost?

The Cost of your meeting comes from the total number of participants and its duration. A standard $53.00 per person per hour is applied to get the meeting Cost.

How does he work out the meeting value?

ROYI assigns the selected outcome/benefit, method & type of meeting, along with the participant expected outcomes a number based on how much they contribute to a well run meeting (based on EventROI research). ROYI then runs his magic meeting converter to come up with an overall Value in dollars.

So, how is the overall ROYI calculated?

ROYI subtracts the meeting Cost from the meeting Value to help you decide if it's worthwhile proceeding.

A positive ROYI is good, it means you are making money.

A negative ROYI probably means you should look at other alternatives, maybe an e-mail would work better to share the information? Maybe you don't need as many participants? Or, maybe you can achieve the outcome you are looking for with a shorter meeting?

How does meeting value impact ROYI?

  • Decision(s) made - The most important reason to meet is to make a decision. Nothing beats the immediacy and interaction of gathering as a group to clarify an objective, flesh out alternatives and evaluate options with balanced listening and advocacy. The more difficult the decision, the greater the importance of a well-run meeting. Recommended meeting length: 25 min
  • Option(s)/Alternative(s) developed - Making progress in solving a problem or analyzing a situation is the second most valuable reason to meet. A complex project requires systematic thought and a careful assessment of risk and reward. So does making a choice with much at stake. Advancing the thinking on a subject may include identifying root causes, establishing milestones, evaluating options and identifying critical success factors Recommended meeting length: 1.5 hrs
  • Information gathered - Maximizing the quality of feedback and/or ideas requires face-to-face encounters. The dynamics of group interaction can stimulate thinking or prompt a person to raise a concern that might otherwise go unmentioned. Separating gathering input from decision making also reduces the time people spend trying to influence others and increases the numbers of options for consideration Recommended meeting length: 55 min
  • Teambuilding - Increasing a team’s capacity may involve skill-building, clarifying roles and responsibilities, improving communications, and more. Capacity-building refers to a dedicated meeting devoted solely to training or productivity improvement. For example, building capacity would include developing employee listening skills or problem-solving abilities
  • Knowledge shared - A meeting just to share information is generally a waste of time in today’s interconnected work environment, and it is listed here – in last place – to make that point. Except in fast-paced, dynamic environments, information can usually be delivered before or in place of a meeting

How does the number of participants impact ROYI?

The number of participants is used to calculate the Cost of the meeting. Ensure that the right people attend by asking:

  • Can they apply what they learn to the benefit of the stakeholder?
  • Are they learning something new to assist them in their actions?
  • Are they sharing knowledge in the meeting to the benefit of the stakeholder?
